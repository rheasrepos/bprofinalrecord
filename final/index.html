Why a New Record?

In 1977, Carl Sagan and his team placed a golden phonograph record aboard the Voyager spacecrafts — a gold-plated copper disc of sounds and images chosen to portray life and culture on Earth for any intelligent extraterrestrial life that might one day encounter it.

Almost 50 years later, the original record reflects its era: western-centric, scientifically limited by what we then knew about the cosmos, and silent on nearly everything that has happened since. This project is a reimagining for the 21st century.

— Ha Do & Rhea Madhogarhia
